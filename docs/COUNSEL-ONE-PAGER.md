## What it is

VoteRight is a nonpartisan civic-information platform, currently piloting in Montgomery
County, Maryland. Voters state their own policy priorities in their own words; candidates
for county office are matched against those priorities using **sourced positions and
public voting records only** — never editorial opinion, never AI-generated claims about a
person. Nothing about a real, named candidate is ever published without a citation to a
public record (an official roll-call vote, a campaign statement, a government filing).

## What's already built and live

The platform runs on real 2026 Montgomery County data today: the actual candidates for
every countywide and district office, 1,925 real recorded council votes, and real
campaign-finance filings — all publicly viewable, though the URL is not yet promoted. No
alignment score, integrity claim, or characterization of any real candidate has been
published. That's exactly what this consultation is about.

## The one question that matters most today

**May we publicly display alignment scores — how well a candidate's record matches a
voter's stated priorities — for real candidates?** The scoring engine and its safeguards
are fully built:

- Scores display as **labeled bands** ("strong match," "leans opposed"), never raw
  numeric scores presented as precise measurement.
- Every input is a **human-confirmed citation** to an actual public record — a recorded
  vote, a campaign statement — never an inference or an AI-generated characterization.
- If a candidate hasn't taken enough public positions to score fairly, the platform
  **says so explicitly** and withholds a score rather than guess.
- A **standing audit program** periodically checks the scoring isn't systematically
  biased toward any party or ideology, with published results.

Given that design, is displaying these scores a defamation or election-law problem under
Maryland law — and if so, what specifically would need to change?

## Second question, if time allows

We've drafted a policy questionnaire to send **identically to every candidate in a race,
on the same day**, with answers published verbatim and a standing right of reply. Any
coordination or campaign-finance concern with a civic platform doing this?
